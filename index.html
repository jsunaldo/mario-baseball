🎮 League Overview

The Mario Superstar Baseball League is a head-to-head baseball season played on Mario Superstar Baseball for the Nintendo GameCube. Two owners — Jason and Dan — each manage a roster of 27 players drafted from the game's character pool. They compete across a full 162-game regular season followed by a best-of-7 World Series.

This is Season 2 of the league. Every game is played on-console, with results, lineups, and player statistics tracked here.

🎫 The Draft
  • Format: Snake draft (pick order reverses each round)
  • Total Picks: 54 (27 per owner)
  • Roster Size: 27 players each
  • The full player pool consists of 54 characters from the game. Every character is drafted — no free agents remain.
  • Once drafted, rosters are locked for the entire season.
⚾ Regular Season
  • Length: 162 games
  • Stadiums: 6 stadiums rotate through the season:
    Mario Stadium, Peach Garden, Wario Palace, Yoshi Park, DK Jungle, Bowser Castle
  • Stadium Rotation: The schedule runs in 18-game cycles. Within each cycle, 3 consecutive games are played at each of the 6 stadiums before moving to the next.
  • Home / Away: Home team alternates each game within a stadium set. The starting home team also alternates between stadium sets so neither owner has a permanent home-field advantage.
  • Early Ending: The owners may agree to end the regular season early once all playoff seeds are clinched. The season record stands as played and the World Series begins immediately.
🧮 Lineups
  • Game Roster: Each team fields 9 players per game out of their 27-man roster.
  • Selection: Each owner chooses 6 players, then 3 additional players are randomly selected from the remaining healthy bench. This ensures variety — you can't run the same 9 every game.
  • Starting Pitcher (SP): After the 9-man lineup is set, each owner must designate one of their 9 as the Starting Pitcher.
  • Pitcher Rest Rule: A player who was the Starting Pitcher cannot pitch in any capacity (SP or relief) for the next 2 games. This forces pitcher rotation and prevents overuse.
  • Injured Players: Players currently on the injury list are unavailable and cannot be selected for lineups.
  • Batting Order: Owners arrange their 9 players into their preferred batting order when entering stats.
⚾ Game Rules
  • All games are played on-console in Mario Superstar Baseball on the Nintendo GameCube.
  • No Ties: Every game must have a winner. If the game is tied after regulation, extra innings are played until a winner is decided.
  • MVP: After each game, the game automatically selects a Most Valuable Player. The MVP is logged as part of the game result.
  • Stadiums: Games are played at the stadium assigned by the schedule. No overrides are allowed.
🩹 Injuries
  • Injury Roll: After each game, an injury roll is performed.
  • Chance: There is a 15% chance an injury occurs (configurable).
  • Selection: If an injury triggers, a random team is selected, then a random player from that team's 9-man game lineup is selected to be injured.
  • Duration: The injured player misses between 1 and 30 games (randomly determined).
  • Recovery: The player returns to availability once their injury duration has passed. For example, a player injured in Game 20 for 5 games returns for Game 25.
  • Impact: Injured players cannot be selected for any lineup during their recovery period.
  • Injuries can also be added or removed manually by the league commissioners.
🏆 Postseason — World Series
  • After all 162 regular season games are completed, the season culminates in the World Series.
  • Format: Best-of-7 series. First owner to win 4 games is crowned champion.
  • Home Field: The owner with the better regular season record has home-field advantage for Games 1, 2, 6, & 7. The other owner is home for Games 3, 4, & 5.
  • Stadium Selection: The regular season winner selects their home stadium first. The other owner then selects their home stadium. Each team plays their home games at their chosen stadium.
  • World Series games follow the same lineup and injury rules as the regular season.
📈 Stats Tracked

Batting

  • H — Hits
  • AB — At-Bats
  • AVG — Batting Average (computed from H & AB)
  • RBI — Runs Batted In
  • HR — Home Runs
  • SB — Stolen Bases
  • SPEC — Specials Used (batting)
  • BIG — Big Plays (defensive highlights)

Pitching

  • IP — Innings Pitched (.1 = one out, .2 = two outs)
  • HA — Hits Allowed
  • K — Strikeouts
  • RA — Runs Allowed
  • ERA — Earned Run Average (computed from RA & IP)
  • PS — Specials Used (pitching)
  • SP — Starting Pitcher designation
  • RP — Relief Pitcher (auto-assigned if pitched but not SP)
